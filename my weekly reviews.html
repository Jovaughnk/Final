Drake's Iceman Review

After launching his rollout in July 2025 Drake releases Iceman and this album is Drake letting the people that went against him a couple years ago that he's still standing strong and calling a few of them out a few of them being Rick Ross, DJ Khaled, J Cole, and Kendrick Lamar. I only listened to the album a couple of times but my favorite ones from the album is Janice STFU, Make Them cry, Burning Bridges, and Make Them remember. Drake has 3 songs off the album that seem to carry the same them which is Make them Cry, Make them Pay, and Make them remember which he addresses all the stuff that happened during the kendrick beef.

Iceman isn't just a album, its also drake becoming more colder to the industry after everything that's happened to him and says in the last track make them remember that the Drake from 2009 that everybody misses isn't going to be seen again after expericing everything thats has happened to him in the industry.

Baby Keem Casino Review

Baby Keem Drops his sophmore album Casino, the album has 11 songs which by the end of the album leaving you feeling you wish there were more songs on the album which i feel is a good thing. Casino is more of a introspective album about baby keems life before the fame and after.

Songs such as No Security which is the first song off the album sets the tone of what the album is going to be about which is Keem talking about his life especially the times when he was younger and dealing with a mother who had a alcohol and drug addiction leading to her not being able to take care of him and leaving Keem with his grandmother. Baby Keem talks about his grandmother and living on the streets with his mother in other songs off Casino.

Even though Baby keem has taken a more introspective route on Casino he still has song which catches your attention at the perfect time when you feel like it starts to become stale. An example of this is after the first track Keem comes with a catchy turn up song in the albums name but also a song Casino. These two songs i feel let you know what this album is going to be.

My personal thoughts about Baby Keem before Casino came out was that he was a bit overhyped after releasing his first album I didn't really like how his voice sounded on medolic blue, but after listening to Casino I understand the hype. Hopefully Baby Keem is able to release new songs in the future to keep his momentum going.

J Cole's The Fall Off Review

Since April 2018 after dropping KOD and having a song off the album called "1985 (Intro To The Fall Off)". The Fall Off has been a long awaited album among J cole and hip hop fans. J cole has deemed this his last album that he'll be doing and going the Jay-z route where he'll only drop a song or album unless he feels like it not because he's under obligation.

How I honestly felt about the Fall Off after listening to it a couple times was that was it? Why I thought that originally was because like everyone else i was hyped for the fall off and after listening to a snippet that j cole posted on instagram i had high expectations for the album which i normally keep my expectations about anything i go into level headed but J cole is a top 3 of mines that I listen too so i couldn't help but set high expectations for it. But as a continued to listen to more of the songs off the album I began to understand why he made the album the way he did.

After being hyped from the snippet that J cole posted on IG which was from the song Who TF IZ U. After listening to the song for the first time I was kind of disappointed because I thought he was doing something different like using a 2000's flow but he ended using more of a eminem type flow which eminem is also in my top 3 but I perferred he expiermented with his flow on his verses. But I like the hook of the song because it made mereminisce of a lil jon or mike jones track they would use on their song. The last verse on the song though I enjoyed i which he used that energy and flow for the entire song.

After talking about my first couple of listens and critiques of mines that I had about the Fall Off after a few months of being out and listening to it I now understand why he made the Fall Off the way he did. My first time listening to J coles first three mixtapes that he released which was The Come Up, The Warm Up, Friday Night Lights and even the offseason they were more about J cole wanting to make it as a rapper, becoming one of the best rappers in hip hop, finally making it mainstream and his life during the fame, and steady trying to improve himself as rapper and not settling for complacency so he can remain one of the biggest rappers in the game. Those were him chasing greatness but now that he's great and been great for all these years, he has nothing left to chase or prove to anyone. J cole also knows he can't stay at the top forever which is why he made Lonley at the top. The fall off from my perspective is J cole finally feeling content with everything that has happened in his life and is okay with falling off as a rapper. Disc 1 off the album is a late 20's J cole still chasing greatness and other temptations as a rapper and he's having a hard time trying to figure out if he should become this way or another and disc 2 is j cole realizing the things he's chasing is just external and internal validation and the best thing to have around is family and friends. Songs I like off the album are Safety, Poor Thang, Legacy, Bombs in the ville/Hit the gas, Lonely at the top, 39 intro, The villest ft Erykah Badu, I love her again, Life sentence, Only you ft Burna Boy, and Quik stop.
